I am a PhD student in EECS at UC Berkeley, advised by Alyosha Efros and Moritz Hardt. I work in machine learning.

Previously, I was an undergraduate student at Cornell University, where I worked with Kilian Weinberger.

My current research direction is training a different model for each test instance. I call this test-time training.

For a complete list of papers, please see my Google Scholar.

Collaborations

I'm always looking for talented undergraduate and master's students to work with. From my past experience, successful projects usually take 20-40 hours a week for the junior collaborator, and a mindset of prioritizing research over everything else. If this sounds like a good fit, please email me, I usually reply on the same day.
